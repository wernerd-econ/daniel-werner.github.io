Working Papers

  • Crime and the Classroom: The Effect of Cartel Violence on School Dropout in Mexico
    Daniel Werner
    Uses a shift-share IV leveraging Colombian cocaine seizures and municipality distance to the Pacific Coast to identify the causal effect of homicide rates on school dropout among 12–18 year olds.
  • Political Alignment in Federal Disaster Relief
    Daniel Werner
    Studies whether counties politically aligned with the sitting president receive a larger share of FEMA Public Assistance funds relative to damage, and whether this effect concentrates in election years. Finds alignment premium disappears for lame-duck presidents, consistent with a vote-maximization mechanism.
